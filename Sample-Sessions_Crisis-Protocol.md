### Part 1: Sample Session Excerpts

#### Session 1: The Science of Survival (How Trauma Gets Built In)
**Session Goal:** To normalize trauma responses as biological survival mechanisms rather than character flaws.

*   **The "Lizard Brain" vs. The "Thinking Brain":** Explaining the Autonomic Nervous System in plain language.
*   **The Big Three:** Fight, Flight, and Freeze. How these kept you alive in the past but cause problems in the present.
*   **Key Concept:** "Your body didn't make a mistake; it made a choice to survive."

#### Session 9: Shame vs. Responsibility (The Internal Battlefield)
**Session Goal:** To distinguish between toxic shame ("I am bad") and healthy accountability ("I did something that caused harm").

*   **Check-In (5–7 min):** Grounding exercise: “Name one thing your body is feeling right now and one thing you see in the room.”
*   **Core Teaching (15 min):** 
    *   Shame says: “I am worthless / broken / evil.”
    *   Responsibility says: “I made a choice that hurt people or myself, and I can learn from it.”
*   **Group Activity (20 min):** Shame vs. Responsibility Sort. Participants categorize real-world scenarios to practice separating identity from actions.
*   **Closing:** Reinforcing that accountability is the first step toward freedom from shame.

### Part 2: Crisis & Safety Protocol (For Peer Facilitators)

**Purpose:** To provide peer facilitators with clear, defensible steps when a participant discloses suicidal thoughts, self-harm, or emotional crisis.

**Always Remember:** You are not a therapist. Your job is to keep the person safe and get them to the proper level of professional help.

#### Step-by-Step Protocol

**Step 1: Immediate Safety Assessment (Ask Directly)**
*   “Are you thinking about harming yourself or ending your life right now?”
*   “Do you have a plan? Do you have the means (gun, pills, etc.)?”

**Step 2: Risk Levels & Actions**

| Risk Level | Description | Actions to Take |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Risk** | Active plan + means + intent | Stay with the person. Do not leave them alone. Call 911 or facility security/medical immediately. Notify program supervisor. |
| **Moderate Risk** | Thoughts but no current plan | Ask: “What has helped you stay safe in the past?” Help them make a safety plan for the next 24 hours. Connect to crisis line (988) or on-site counselor. |
| **Low Risk** | General hopelessness, no active intent | Acknowledge the pain. Reinforce group support and program tools. Offer to help them talk to a counselor or sponsor. |

**Step 3: Post-Incident Documentation**
1.  Document the time, what was said, and actions taken.
2.  Inform your supervisor within 1 hour.
3.  Check on the participant the next day if possible.

**Emergency Contacts:**
*   **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text 988 (Available 24/7)
*   **Local Emergency:** 911
