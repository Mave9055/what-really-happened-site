### Sample Session Excerpt: Session 9 – Shame vs. Responsibility

#### Session Goals
*   Help participants distinguish between shame (“I am bad”) and responsibility (“I did something that caused harm”).
*   Reduce toxic shame that drives recidivism, addiction, and isolation.
*   Increase personal accountability without self-destruction.

#### Session Outline (60–75 minutes)

1.  **Check-In (5–7 min)**
    *   Grounding exercise: “Name one thing your body is feeling right now and one thing you see in the room.”
2.  **Core Teaching (15 min)**
    *   Shame says: “I am worthless / broken / evil.”
    *   Responsibility says: “I made a choice that hurt people or myself, and I can learn from it.”
    *   Trauma often turns normal guilt into overwhelming shame.
    *   Example: “Stealing a car because I was desperate vs. ‘I am a thief and always will be.’”
3.  **Group Activity – Shame vs Responsibility Sort (20 min)**
    *   Facilitator reads scenarios. Group discusses and sorts them into Shame, Responsibility, or Both.
    *   (Facilitator script and 8 example scenarios provided in full manual)
4.  **Personal Reflection (10 min)**
    *   Quiet writing prompt:
        *   “One thing I have carried shame about is…”
        *   “If I separated the shame from the responsibility, what would change?”
5.  **Closing & Safety Check (5–8 min)**
    *   One-word check-out
    *   Reminder of crisis protocol (see below)

#### Facilitator Notes
Watch for participants who shut down or become highly agitated. Shame work is powerful but can be destabilizing. Use the Crisis & Safety Protocol immediately if needed.

### Crisis & Safety Protocol (For Peer Facilitators)

**Purpose:** To give peer facilitators clear, simple, defensible steps when someone discloses suicidal thoughts, self-harm, or emotional crisis during or after a session.

**Always Remember:** You are not a therapist. Your job is to keep the person safe and get them to the proper level of help.

#### Step-by-Step Protocol

**Step 1: Immediate Safety Assessment (Ask Directly)**
*   “Are you thinking about harming yourself or ending your life right now?”
*   “Do you have a plan? Do you have the means (gun, pills, etc.)?”

**Step 2: Risk Levels & Actions**

| Risk Level | Description | Actions to Take |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Risk** | Active plan + means + intent | Stay with the person. Do not leave them alone. Call 911 or facility security/medical immediately. Notify program supervisor. |
| **Moderate Risk** | Thoughts but no current plan | Ask: “What has helped you stay safe in the past?” Help them make a safety plan for the next 24 hours. Connect to crisis line (988) or on-site counselor. |
| **Low Risk** | General hopelessness, no active intent | Acknowledge the pain: “What you’re carrying sounds incredibly heavy.” Reinforce group support and program tools. Offer to help them talk to a counselor or sponsor. |

**After Any Crisis Disclosure**
1.  Document what happened (time, what was said, actions taken).
2.  Inform your supervisor within 1 hour.
3.  Check on the participant the next day if possible.
4.  Debrief with your co-facilitator or supervisor.

**988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** – Available 24/7: Call or text 988  
**Local Emergency:** 911

#### Facilitator Self-Care
Debrief after difficult sessions. You are not expected to carry this alone.
